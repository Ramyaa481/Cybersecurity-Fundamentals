### Introduction
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, systems, and data from unauthorized access, cyberattacks, and damage. 
It helps ensure the safety and reliability of digital information.

## The CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is the foundation of cybersecurity and consists of three key principles:

### 1. Confidentiality
Confidentiality ensures that sensitive information is accessible only to authorized users.

Examples:
- Password protection
- Encryption
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

### 2. Integrity
Integrity ensures that data remains accurate, complete, and unaltered by unauthorized users.

Examples:
- Hashing
- Digital signatures
- Access controls

### 3. Availability
Availability ensures that systems and data are accessible when needed.

Examples:
- Regular backups
- Disaster recovery plans
- Redundant servers

## Types of Hackers

### White Hat Hackers
Ethical hackers who help organizations identify and fix security vulnerabilities.

### Black Hat Hackers
Malicious hackers who exploit vulnerabilities for personal gain or to cause damage.

### Gray Hat Hackers
Hackers who may access systems without permission but generally do not have malicious intentions.

### Script Kiddies
Individuals with limited technical knowledge who use pre-built hacking tools.

### Hacktivists
Hackers who conduct cyberattacks to support political or social causes.

### State-Sponsored Hackers
Government-backed hackers involved in cyber espionage or cyber warfare.

## Common Cyber Threats

### Malware
Malware is malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to systems.

Types:
- Virus
- Worm
- Trojan
- Spyware

Impact:
- Data theft
- System damage
- Reduced performance

### Phishing
Phishing is a social engineering attack that tricks users into revealing sensitive information through fake emails, messages, or websites.

Impact:
- Credential theft
- Identity theft
- Financial loss

### Ransomware
Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts files and demands payment for decryption.

Impact:
- Loss of access to data
- Business disruption
- Financial damage

## Impact on Systems

| Threat | Impact |
|----------|----------|
| Malware | Damages files, steals data, slows systems |
| Phishing | Compromises user accounts and sensitive information |
| Ransomware | Makes data inaccessible and disrupts operations |

## Cybersecurity Best Practices

- Use strong passwords.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication.
- Keep software updated.
- Avoid suspicious links and attachments.
- Install antivirus software.
- Regularly back up important data.

## Conclusion

Cybersecurity plays a vital role in protecting digital assets and information. Understanding the CIA Triad, different types of hackers, 
and common cyber threats such as malware, phishing, and ransomware helps individuals and organizations improve their security posture and reduce cyber risks.
